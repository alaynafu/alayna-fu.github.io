ChIPMOD1 is a computational model that simulates the dynamics of RNA polymerase II during transcription. Updating ChIPMOD to match findings from recent studies, we observed how promoter-proximal pausing affects RNAPII density during transcription. Adjusting how the algorithm calculates the movements of the polymerase and varying the position-specific parameters of the polymerase, we were able to observe that with parameter optimization we can simulate results that reflect those of experimental data. In eukaryotic cells, RNAPII promoter-proximal pausing is an important regulation step of the transcription process. However, the physical dynamics of RNAPII are not yet well understood. Computational modeling of RNAPII can allow researchers to understand how certain parameters affect the overall process of transcription and predict behaviors of RNAPII. The objective of the study is to see if the dynamics of RNA polymerase during transcription can be numerically simulated.
